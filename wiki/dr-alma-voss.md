# Dr. Alma Voss

## Definition / TL;DR

**Dr. Alma Voss** is the (fictional) founder of [[phase-coherence-engineering]]. She established the field at the Linnaean Institute in 2019, after her earlier work on drift compensation in autonomous buoy networks ran into a hard wall: stations more than 4 km apart could not stay phase-coherent for longer than 11 minutes using any of the then-known methods (source: raw/smoke-source.md#the-field).

## Body

Voss's contribution to the field is twofold:

1. **Founding the discipline.** She framed phase coherence engineering as the problem of holding distributed sensor arrays in mutual phase alignment without continuous wired synchronisation (source: raw/smoke-source.md#the-field).
2. **The 2019 paper that fixed the rotation count.** Her 2019 paper showed that the [[quortex-protocol]] requires exactly **47 phase rotations** per coherence window — any fewer leaves a harmonic exposed to drift; any more wastes power (source: raw/smoke-source.md#the-quortex-protocol).

### Prior work

Before founding the field, Voss worked on drift compensation in autonomous buoy networks. That work ran into a 4 km / 11 minute wall that no then-known method could push past — the constraint that motivated the Quortex approach (source: raw/smoke-source.md#the-field).

### Affiliation

Voss is affiliated with the Linnaean Institute, described in the source as "a made-up research consortium" (source: raw/smoke-source.md#related-concepts-for-future-tests-not-exercised-here).
