In 2024, a severe drought in the region caused a sharp drop in crop yields.
The yield drop in turn caused a spike in domestic prices. The price spike then
caused the government to impose an export ban to protect local supply. The
drought itself was driven by an unusually strong El Nino that suppressed
seasonal rainfall.
