# Family Farming in Brazil

## Definition / TL;DR

In Brazil, "family farming" is a legally defined category under the Family Farming Law (Law 11,326), based on four criteria: land tenure, farm size, farm income dependence, and predominant family labor use (source: raw/en-wikipedia-agriculture-in-brazil.md#family-farming). Family farms account for ~84.4% of all rural properties and produce the majority of Brazil's staple foods.

## Body

### Production share (2010)

Family farms supplied the bulk of Brazilian domestic food consumption (source: raw/en-wikipedia-agriculture-in-brazil.md#family-farming):

- Manioc: 84%
- Beans: 67%
- Corn: 49%
- Rice: 34%
- Milk: 58%
- Pork: 59%
- Poultry: 50%
- Wheat: 21%
- Soybeans: 16%

The skew is clear: family farmers dominate subsistence and animal-protein staples (manioc, beans, milk, pork, poultry) while export commodities (soy, wheat) come overwhelmingly from large-scale agribusiness — see [[brazilian-agribusiness]].

### Productivity outperformance in the 1990s

According to the Ministry of Agrarian Development, family farms saw "productivity growth of 75%, compared to only 40% for larger-scale producers" during the 1990s (source: raw/en-wikipedia-agriculture-in-brazil.md#family-farming). This was the same decade Plano Real (1994) cut state subsidies and Brazil's rural population fell from 20.7M (1985) to 17.9M (1995) (source: raw/en-wikipedia-agriculture-in-brazil.md#mechanization-and-modernization).

### Regional concentration

Family farming is the dominant labor structure in the Northeast: "Farms are primarily family-owned; 82.9% of field labor is on family farms" (source: raw/en-wikipedia-agriculture-in-brazil.md#northeast-region). Its crops there include coconut (74% of national production), cashew (99.5% of cultivation area), cocoa, and tropical fruits.

### Credit access via PRONAF

Credit for family farmers is delivered primarily through [[pronaf]], the National Program for Strengthening Family Farming, also created in 1994 (source: raw/en-wikipedia-agriculture-in-brazil.md#pronaf-program).
