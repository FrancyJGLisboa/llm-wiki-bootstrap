# PRONAF

## Definition / TL;DR

PRONAF (_Programa Nacional de Fortalecimento da Agricultura Familiar_, National Program for Strengthening Family Farming) was created in 1994 to address the credit needs of Brazilian family farmers (source: raw/en-wikipedia-agriculture-in-brazil.md#pronaf-program). It is the principal financial instrument supporting [[family-farming-brazil]].

## Body

PRONAF's lending volume scaled rapidly in its first decade and a half: "Loans written out to family farmers through PRONAF rose from US$1 billion in 2000 to an estimated US$5.8 billion in 2008" — a ~5.8x increase in eight years (source: raw/en-wikipedia-agriculture-in-brazil.md#pronaf-program).

The program was launched the same year as Plano Real (1994), which cut general agricultural subsidies and shifted financing of agriculture to the market — see the mechanization-and-modernization context in [[agriculture-in-brazil-summary]]. PRONAF preserved a targeted credit channel for the family-farm tier even as broader subsidies were withdrawn (source: raw/en-wikipedia-agriculture-in-brazil.md#mechanization-and-modernization).
